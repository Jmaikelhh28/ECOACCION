Latest News

New Solar Panel Project in Hospitals

We have launched a support program for hospitals using sustainable energy. We are installing solar panels that guarantee power supply in critical areas, reducing costs and improving medical care. This project helps reduce the environmental footprint and ensures stable energy during vital moments.

Volunteer Campaign

Over 5,000 young volunteers, in collaboration with EcoAction, have been trained as solar panel installers. The campaign not only creates job opportunities but also strengthens environmental awareness in communities, generating a positive social and ecological impact.

Partnerships with Universities for Solar Research

EcoAction has signed agreements with universities to develop more efficient and accessible solar technologies for vulnerable communities. These partnerships promote innovation, the development of new materials, and smart systems that maximize the use of renewable energy.

Solar Panels in Rural Communities

The installation of solar systems in 12 rural communities has been successfully completed, benefiting over 800 families. This access to clean energy improves quality of life, supporting education, healthcare, and local economic activities with a reliable and sustainable energy source.

Free Solar Energy Training

EcoAction is launching a free online training program on the installation and maintenance of solar panels, with official certification for unemployed youth. This program strengthens participants’ technical skills, facilitates job placement, and promotes the use of renewable energy in various communities.

Positive Environmental Impact Report

The use of solar panels has reduced CO₂ emissions by 35% in the community centers supported by EcoAction. This result demonstrates the effectiveness of clean energy in combating climate change and improving the environmental health of the supported communities.
